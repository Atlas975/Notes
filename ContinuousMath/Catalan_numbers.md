## Permutations
> $$\mathcal{P(n,r)} = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$$
$$ n = \text{total number of objects in the set}$$
$$ r = \text{number of choosing objects from the set}$$
## Combinations
- The number of combinations can be represented by the formula:

> $$\mathcal{C(n,r)} = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$$
$$ n = \text{total number of objects in the set}$$
$$ r = \text{number of choosing objects from the set}$$
